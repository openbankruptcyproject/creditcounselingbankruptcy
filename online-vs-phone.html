Online vs Phone Credit Counseling

Both work equally well -- choose whichever is most convenient for your schedule

Online Credit Counseling

The most popular option. You complete the course on a computer or smartphone at your own pace. Most online courses take 60-90 minutes and include:

  • A budget analysis based on your income and expenses
  • A review of your debts and financial situation
  • Discussion of alternatives to bankruptcy
  • A proposed debt management plan (which you are not obligated to follow)
  • A brief quiz or review section

Upon completion, you receive your certificate immediately by email or as a downloadable PDF.

Phone Counseling

If you prefer to speak with a counselor directly, most agencies offer phone sessions. The call typically lasts 45-90 minutes. The counselor will:

  • Ask about your income, expenses, debts, and assets
  • Discuss your financial situation and potential alternatives
  • Issue a certificate by email or mail after the session

Phone counseling can be particularly helpful if you have questions about your financial situation or want to discuss options before filing.

In-Person Counseling

Some agencies offer in-person sessions, though these have become less common since the pandemic. Call ahead to check availability if you prefer face-to-face interaction.

Tips

  • Have your pay stubs, bank statements, and a list of debts handy before starting
  • The course is a legal requirement, not a gatekeeping mechanism -- completing it does not prevent you from filing
  • If the agency suggests a debt management plan, you are not required to accept it
  • Complete the course at least a few days before your planned filing date, not the same day
