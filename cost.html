How Much Does Credit Counseling Cost?

Most courses cost $15-50, and fee waivers are available if you cannot afford it

Home›How Much Does Bankruptcy Credit Counseling Cost?

Typical Fee Ranges

Course TypeTypical CostFee Waiver Available?
Pre-filing credit counseling$15-50Yes
Post-filing debtor education$10-50Yes
Combined (both courses, same provider)$25-75Yes

What Is Included

The fee covers:

  • The full credit counseling or debtor education session
  • A budget analysis and personalized recommendations
  • The official certificate required for your court filing
  • No additional charges for phone vs online delivery (in most cases)

Be wary of agencies charging significantly more than $50 for either course. Some less reputable agencies bundle unnecessary services with the required course to inflate the price.

Fee Waivers for Low Income

If you cannot afford the fee, approved agencies are required to provide services at reduced fees or for free. Under EOUST guidelines, agencies must consider your ability to pay and offer reduced rates or waivers based on income. To request a fee waiver:

  1. Ask the agency directly when you enroll
  2. Explain your financial situation - if you qualify for a fee waiver on the court filing fee, you almost certainly qualify for a reduced counseling fee
  3. If one agency will not waive the fee, try another - different agencies have different policies

Avoid Scams

  • Only use EOUST-approved agencies. Check the list at justice.gov/ust.
  • Do not pay more than $75 for both courses combined. If someone is charging $100+ for a single course, look elsewhere.
  • Your attorney should not be charging you separately for the counseling course. If they do, ask why.
